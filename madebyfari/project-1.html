Sòrò Sókè

Medium: Merch Design

Date: October 2024

This typography-focused merch project was inspired by the first protest I ever attended in London in October 2021, held in response to police brutality in Nigeria. Sorosoke, which means “speak up” in Yoruba, became a powerful mantra that I brought into my design process.

The hand-drawn letterforms and accent marks reflect both the energy of the slogan and its cultural weight. As I developed the project, I added symbolic elements to give the design more depth: a fist rising from the “k,” an evil eye in the “o,” and a figure speaking in the shape of the “e.”
