AfriCOBRA Poster

Medium: Poster

Date: November 2024

This poster is a typographic tribute to AfriCOBRA, the African Commune of Bad Relevant Artists. Founded in 1968 during the Black Arts Movement, the collective called for a new Black aesthetic rooted in community, pride, and political urgency. I pulled direct language from the AfriCOBRA manifesto to shape the composition, using text as both message and material. The design plays with angle, scale, and weight to create a sense of movement and depth. Bold, overlapping typography and a limited palette of red, yellow, and black reflect the energy and visual language of the collective’s work, especially the iconic Wall of Respect. This piece channels the manifesto’s values and turns them into a visual statement about identity, resistance, and joy.
